### Duplicate Files (candidates for consolidation)

- **trading/utils/cache_manager.py** vs **trading/services/cache_manager.py**: Both define a `CacheManager` class. `trading.utils.cache_manager` is the main export (used by trading/utils/__init__.py and decorators). `trading.services.cache_manager` is used by `trading/services/quant_gpt.py` for service-level caching. **Recommendation:** Keep `trading/utils/cache_manager.py` as the canonical implementation; refactor `quant_gpt` to use it or a thin wrapper to avoid two implementations.
- **config/config.py** vs **config/app_config.py** vs **config/primary_config.py**: Not duplicates—different roles. `config.py` is a simplified interface; `app_config.py` holds AppConfig and loading; `primary_config.py` is the documented single entry point that delegates to `get_config()`. **Recommendation:** Keep all three; ensure primary_config remains the single documented entry.
- **llm/llm_summary.py** vs **agents/llm/llm_summary.py**: Different behavior (strategy commentary vs market summary with SummaryConfig). **Recommendation:** Keep both; consider renaming or moving one into a shared `llm_summary` package to avoid confusion.
- **utils/performance_metrics.py** vs **trading/utils/performance_metrics.py**: Likely overlapping metrics helpers; need a single source of truth for performance metrics used by both app and trading. **Recommendation:** Audit call sites; consolidate into one module (e.g. trading.utils) and re-export from utils if needed for backward compatibility.
